Description of the Course

This course is the first semester of a two-semester sequel on quantum theory, stressing principles.
Topics covered include: Hilbert spaces, observables, uncertainty relations, eigenvalue problems and methods
for solutions thereof, time-evolution in the Schrödinger, Heisenberg, and interaction pictures, connections
between classical and quantum mechanics, angular momentum, density operators, and quantum measurement.
The first semester focuses on basic theory while the second semester (PHYS 660) reviews more advanced
theory applications. The first semester covers in particular wave functions and the Schrödinger equation;
the mathematical formalism of quantum mechanics, including Hilbert space, Dirac notation, and operators;
the hydrogen atom; angular momentum and spin.
